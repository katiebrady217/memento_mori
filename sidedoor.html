EXIT

Backing away from the grand front steps you walk round through the kitchen gardens at the side of the house, the now meagre offerings a shadow of their former prime when this house used to bustle with life. You step into a raucous kitchen on high alert. The staff trying to prepare a meal for the homecoming of the master of the house. The Lord and Lady would never miss a chance for a party and were planning this for weeks. Advanced notice never made things easier however. The produce had been delivered late, the staff were down serving boys and their was worry the wine left in the cellar wouldn’t be sufficient.

In the corner stood an oasis of calm. Carrying the needlework the housekeeper had requested from her private rooms stood the new maid. No more than a girl really but the mistress had been impressed by the fact that nothing seemed to faze her; not the stress of the kitchens or the tight deadlines tasks needed to be completed by. Everything was taken in stride. Looking a little closer and her employer might have seen what lay beneath that single-minded focus but for now she was simply a good, honest worker.
